Zucchini

Cucurbita pepo

Zucchini is a summer crop that grows best in an temperate arid environment.

The plant thrives in temperatures between 70 to 95 degrees Fahrenheit. Temperatures must remain above 60 degrees Fahrenheit for the plant to germinate.

Zucchini needs to be planted in soil with high levels or organic matter. It also needs to be planted in areas with 6 to 8 hours of direct sunlight. Zucchini need moist soil, and around 1 inch per week of rain.
